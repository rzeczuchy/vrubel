The art of Mikhail Vrubel

Mikail Vrubel [Михаил Александрович Врубель] - the 19th-20th century Russian painter

Mikhail Vrubel

Born a fragile and unhealthy child, Mikhail Vrubel displayed artistic inclinations early, but only decided to pursue a career as an artist in his mid-twenties, when he started attending the Imperial Academy of Arts.

In his work he took inspiration from religious art, Russian romantic painters (such as Ivan Aivazovsky), as well as theatre and literature. Despite obvious talent and some success, Vrubel never graduated from the Academy. Instead he went on to pursue a commercial career as both a religious painter and portretist. His most productive years followed, during which the artists painted some of his most acclaimed works, including the "Demon" series.

His late years were marred by mental illness and depression, and Vrubel was then hardly able to paint. Even so, a couple of his better-known works (like the Six-winged Seraph, 1904) were produced during this period, indicating his artistic senses were still sharp at times. He left behind a legacy that was defiant and unforgettable.
